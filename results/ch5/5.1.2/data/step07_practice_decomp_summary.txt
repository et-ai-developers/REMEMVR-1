================================================================================
PRACTICE EFFECTS DECOMPOSITION SUMMARY - RQ 5.1.2
================================================================================

RESEARCH QUESTION:
  Does T1→T2 trajectory reflect practice gains or genuine forgetting?

METHODOLOGY:
  Dual-phase model: theta ~ Time_within_phase * Phase + (1 | UID)
  Phase 1 (Practice): T1→T2 (first retest)
  Phase 2 (Forgetting): T2→T4 (subsequent tests)

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
RESULTS:
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Practice phase slope (T1→T2):   β = -0.003325 ± 0.001031, p = 0.001258
Forgetting phase slope (T2→T4): β = -0.018965 ± 0.003094
Slope difference:                Δβ = 0.015640, p = 0.000002

CONCLUSION: Significant practice effect detected (p < 0.0033)
  Practice PARTIALLY masks forgetting: T1→T2 decline less steep than T2→T4

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
IMPLICATIONS FOR RQ 5.1.3 (AGE EFFECTS):
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
  CRITICAL: Age × Time analysis must account for practice effects
  Younger adults may benefit MORE from practice (processing speed advantage)
  This could create artifact of 'older adults better forgetting rate'
  Recommendation: Test Age × Phase interaction before interpreting Age × Time
